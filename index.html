Impulse, Momentum and Valence Electrons




Impulse is the force that happens after the net force is applied to an object. It is the product of the net force applied to an object and the time of impact.
Momentum is the inertia of an object. It is the product of an object's mass and velocity.
Valence Electrons are the electrons found in the outer layer of an atom. They are crucial in the formation of atomic compounds.
